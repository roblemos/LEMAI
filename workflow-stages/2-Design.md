# D — Design: Structure, Architect & Collect Materials

 **LEMAI: AI-L2 [I1,D2,W3,R2,V1,F2]**  



The Design stage is where ideas are given shape and necessary materials are collected. In writing, this means interviewing sources, outlining, structuring arguments, and planning narrative flow. In software development, it means system architecture, component design, data modeling, and API contracts. The decisions made here constrain everything downstream.

**Note:** Interviews with sources could also be part of ideation, but are included here as the stage at which ideas (Ideate) are made concrete (Work). 

---

| Activity | AI Level | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Sketching an outline or system architecture by hand, referencing documentation | 0 | Entirely human-designed; no AI involvement at any step |
| Asking AI to explain a design pattern to inform a technical decision the human is already making | 1 | AI answers a targeted question; the human applies their own judgment to the design |
| Using AI to review a rough article outline and suggest structural improvements or alternative orderings | 1 | AI provides reactive feedback on a human-created artifact; human retains design ownership |
| Co-creating a system architecture with AI through iterative dialogue, with the human setting constraints and approving each decision | 2 | Human and AI jointly shape the structure; human sets direction and approves each step |
| Asking AI to generate multiple structural outlines for a long-form piece, then selecting and adapting the most appropriate one | 2 | AI expands the option space; human selects and adapts the chosen structure |
| Asking AI to produce a complete system architecture from a short requirements brief | 3 | AI generates the primary design artifact; human evaluates and approves with adjustments |
| Asking AI to generate a full article outline, section structure, and key argument flow from a one-paragraph pitch | 3 | AI drives the structural decisions; human validates the output rather than creating it |
| AI agent takes a high-level engineering goal and autonomously produces a complete architecture with no intermediate human input | 4 | No human involvement in design creation; human receives a completed artifact for approval |
| AI system continuously updates a publication's content structure and editorial templates based on reader engagement data | 4 | Fully AI-native; the design evolves autonomously without human direction |
