# W — Work: Writing & Coding

 **LEMAI: AI-L2 [I1,D2,W3,R2,V1,F2]**  



The Work stage is where the primary artifact is produced — prose is written, code is authored, data pipelines are built. This is typically the most labor-intensive stage and the one where AI assistance is most visibly transformative. The level of AI involvement here has the greatest effect on the character of the output. Particular activities are assigned labels to refer back to them. The labels are typically, workflow stage, type of process (Dev = development, Creat = Creative), AI usage level, and a letter to make the label distinct.

---

| Activity | AI Level | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Writing all copy or code manually without any AI assistance | 0 | Purely human execution; no AI tools involved at any point |
| **W:Dev:1-A** — Asking AI to explain why a specific error is occurring in code and how to fix it | 1 | AI answers a targeted diagnostic question; human writes all code and applies the fix |
| **W:Creat:1-A** — Using AI to suggest rephrasing for a sentence the human has already identified as weak | 1 | AI provides a reactive suggestion on a human-flagged issue; the human decides whether to apply it |
| **W:Dev:2-A** — Pair-coding with AI, where the human writes function signatures and logic stubs and AI fills in implementations for review | 2 | Human and AI co-produce the output; human shapes direction and edits throughout |
| **W:Creat:2-A** — Asking AI to draft a transitional paragraph or section introduction based on a detailed human-written outline point | 2 | AI accelerates execution on a well-specified task; human provides intent and reviews output |
| **W:Dev:3-A** — Using AI to draft a complete module or service based on a spec, with the human's role limited to reviewing and refining the output | 3 | AI produces the primary artifact; human's role is review and editing rather than authoring |
| **W:Creat:3-A** — Giving AI a reporting brief and having it draft the bulk of an article, with the human refining voice and verifying facts | 3 | AI drives the work phase; human validates output after production |
| **W:Dev:4-A** — AI agent receives a task ticket and autonomously writes, tests, and commits code | 4 | Human involvement is limited to task definition and final inspection; AI executes end-to-end |
| **W:Creat:4-A** — AI system generates and publishes content continuously based on configured parameters | 4 | Fully AI-native execution; no human involvement in the work phase itself |
